What is ICT?

Information and Communication Technology (ICT) encompasses the infrastructure and components that enable modern computing. It includes technologies for data storage, processing, and communication.

History of ICT

The concept of ICT emerged in the late 20th century when information technology and telecommunications converged. Early examples of ICT include telegraphs, telephone systems, and radio communication. The digital revolution of the 1970s and 1980s brought personal computers, internet connectivity, and mobile technologies into the ICT framework.

Parts of ICT

ICT is divided into several parts that work together:

  • Hardware: Physical devices like computers, servers, and networking equipment.
  • Software: Operating systems, applications, and tools for processing data.
  • Telecommunications: Technologies like wireless networks, fiber optics, and satellite communications.
  • Data: The information being stored, processed, or transmitted.

Subsystems of ICT

ICT comprises several interconnected subsystems:

  • Data Processing Subsystems: Manage and manipulate data for useful output.
  • Communication Subsystems: Enable data exchange through email, messaging, or video calls.
  • Storage Subsystems: Store data securely and efficiently using cloud or local solutions.
  • Security Subsystems: Protect systems and data from unauthorized access or breaches.

Main Components of ICT

ICT's main components are essential for enabling modern communication and information-sharing:

  • Devices: Computers, smartphones, tablets, servers, and peripherals.
  • Networks: Wired or wireless systems like LAN, WAN, and the internet.
  • Platforms: Software platforms that support business, education, and collaboration, such as Microsoft Office or Google Workspace.
  • Human Resources: Skilled professionals such as IT managers, developers, and engineers.
